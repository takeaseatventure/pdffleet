faq

Paperplane alternative — questions

Is PDFFleet a good Paperplane alternative?

Yes. PDFFleet and Paperplane both render HTML to PDF with the same headless Chromium engine, so output fidelity is identical. PDFFleet's edge is the paid tier: $4/mo for 10,000 PDFs is about 10× cheaper per PDF than Paperplane's $9.99/mo for 2,500. Paperplane's free tier (1,000 conversions/mo) is larger than PDFFleet's (100/mo).

How does PDFFleet's pricing compare to Paperplane?

PDFFleet's paid plan is $4/month for 10,000 PDFs; Paperplane's cheapest paid plan is $9.99/mo for 2,500 conversions (≈$0.004/PDF); its higher tiers are $29.99/mo for 10,000 and $99.99/mo for 50,000. PDFFleet is $4/mo for 10,000 (≈$0.0004/PDF), with a free tier of 100 PDFs/month and a Pro plan at $29/month for 150,000. Prices verified June 2026 — check each provider's current pricing page.

Do I have to rewrite my code to switch from Paperplane?

No. Migrating is essentially a one-line change: point your render request at https://pdffleet.com/v1/pdf with your PDFFleet API key. Your HTML, CSS, and template logic stay the same — PDFFleet accepts inline HTML, a URL, or a named template with JSON data.

Does PDFFleet produce the same quality PDFs as Paperplane?

Yes — both use headless Chromium, which renders full CSS, web fonts, backgrounds, and images at print fidelity. The PDF output is equivalent; only the price differs.

Is there a free tier?

Yes. PDFFleet includes 100 PDFs per month free, forever, with no credit card required.
